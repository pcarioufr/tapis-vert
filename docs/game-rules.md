# Tapis Vert - Top 10 Game Rules

**Tapis Vert** is an online version of the classic **Top 10** party game. Groups of friends give creative answers using secret numbered cards and try to fool (or help) the master who guesses who has which number.

## Quick Start

1. **Join a Game**: Click a room link or scan a QR code
2. **Get Your Card**: You'll receive a secret number from 1 to 10
3. **Listen to the Theme**: The master announces a creative scale
4. **Give Your Answer**: Provide an answer that matches your number's position
5. **Watch the Master Guess**: See if they can figure out who has which number!

## What You Need

- **3+ Players** (best with 4-8)
- **One Master** to create themes and guess
- **Creativity and humor**

## Roles

- **Master**: Creates themes, manages the game, makes guesses
- **Player**: Gets a card, gives answers, reveals at the end
- **Watcher**: Observes and chats but doesn't get cards
- **Visitor**: Unauthenticated — can observe before deciding to play

## Round Structure

### 1. Card Distribution
The master clicks "New Round". Cards 1-10 are shuffled and each **player** receives one secret card. Watchers don't get cards.

### 2. Theme Announcement
The master announces a theme following this format:
> "On a scale from 1 to 10, where 1 is [quality A] and 10 is [quality B], give me [category]"

**Example themes:**
- "1 (most boring) to 10 (most exciting) — weekend activities"
- "1 (least scary) to 10 (most terrifying) — things found in a basement"
- "1 (most useless) to 10 (most essential) — superpowers"
- "1 (most stupid) to 10 (most heroic) — ways to die"

### 3. Answer Phase
Players peek at their card (single click) and give an answer in chat that matches their number's position on the scale.

**Example** for "1 (most boring) to 10 (most exciting) — weekend activities":
- **Card 2**: "Organizing my sock drawer"
- **Card 5**: "Going grocery shopping"
- **Card 8**: "Bungee jumping"
- **Card 10**: "Skydiving while on fire"

### 4. Master Guessing
The master reviews all answers and tries to match each player to their card number.

### 5. Reveal
Players double-click their cards to reveal. Everyone sees how well the master guessed. Discuss, laugh, repeat.

### 6. Next Round
Optionally rotate the master role. New master starts a new round with a fresh theme.

## Card Interactions

| Action | How | Who can | Who sees it |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Peek** | Single click card back | Players and watchers (not masters) | Only you (corner lift) |
| **Flip/Reveal** | Double click your card | Card owner only | Everyone |
| **Score** | Click any card | Masters only | Everyone (score badge) |

**Note:** Masters click cards to open the score selector (1-10 grid). They cannot peek or flip cards. Only the card's owner can flip it.

## Tips for Players

- **Match your number**: Your answer should fit where your card falls on the scale
- **Be creative**: Funny or unexpected answers make the game better
- **Don't be too obvious**: If you have 10, don't always pick the most extreme example
- **Know your group**: Use references your friends will get

## Tips for Masters

- **Clear scales**: Make sure 1 and 10 are clearly defined opposites
- **Good categories**: Pick topics everyone can relate to
- **Mix it up**: Alternate between silly, serious, and absurd themes
- **Take your time**: Think about what each number might look like before guessing

## Getting Started

### Joining
1. Get a room link from a friend (or scan a QR code)
2. Open it in your browser — you're in as a visitor
3. Optional: Log in with a magic code for full features

### Playing
1. Wait for the master to start a new round
2. Click your card to peek at your secret number
3. Give your answer in chat when it's your turn
4. Reveal your card when the master is done guessing

### Being the Master
1. Click "New Round" to deal cards
2. Announce your theme in chat
3. Let players answer one by one
4. Make your guesses
5. Ask everyone to reveal!

## Game Variations

Groups can experiment with twists on the classic rules:
- **Reverse scales**: 10 is worst, 1 is best
- **One word only**: Answers limited to a single word
- **Speed rounds**: Strict time limits
- **Team masters**: Multiple people collaborate on guessing
- **Character mode**: Answer as a specific character or personality
